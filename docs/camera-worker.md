# Camera Worker architecture

The default browser camera path samples at most one RGBA frame at a time, caps the longest side at 960 pixels, and transfers the backing `ArrayBuffer` to a persistent module Worker. The Worker owns one CaptureRouter and engine registry for its lifetime. Upload and camera jobs converge on the same Router result contract.

Generation IDs are carried in Worker requests and responses and suppress results after source switch, restart, orientation reset, or stop. No frame queue exists: a sample is skipped while one frame is active. Stop cancels the active job and terminates the Worker; disposal also releases Router engines. Camera transfer uses a dedicated buffer so the original frame remains owned for immediate same-frame main-thread fallback. Worker construction/crash failure enters degraded main-thread mode with stepped retry delays and a bounded consecutive-restart limit; stable Worker success resets recovery state. Browsers without Worker support use the main-thread path.

Camera scanning starts with the caller scenario (fast by default), tries an expanded previous-result ROI when upright geometry exists, and derives a deterministic `<base-id>.camera-escalated` scenario after a configured miss threshold. Derivation preserves engine order, formats, validators, parsers, output, duplicate policy, failure policy, custom IDs, and extensions; only bounded processing strength increases. Callers may provide an explicit escalation scenario. Result, stop, orientation/source reset, and source switch clear internal track/ROI state. `InternalTrack` is not a public tracked-instance feature.

Torch, zoom, facing mode, preferred resolution, page visibility, track-ended, device switch, and orientation-change hooks are capability-aware. OffscreenCanvas, ImageBitmap, and VideoFrame are detected by the browser benchmark but are not mandatory transfer formats in Alpha.3.
